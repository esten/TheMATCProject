For Linguists (and Interested Non-Linguists)

How did you choose your POS tags?

I slightly modified the tags found in this paper from researchers at Carnegie Mellon University. In general, I chose to get rid of the tags in the paper that were described as 'Twitter/online-specific' and 'Miscellaneous'. I am interested in creating a corpus of text speak/neologisms that may not be annotated anywhere else (or available for free). Emoticons, punctuation, numerals, Twitter user handles*, et cetera, are not linguistically relevant for the purposes of The TwitUrban Dictionary.

If you have questions or comments about why I chose to only tag these certain parts of speech, please contact me. For more information on the part of speech tags, please see my guide.

*Twitter user handles are user names; they show up as @(name) in tweets.

Not currently. I'd love to, though. If you have linguistic expertise in another language and want to help design a MATC-type of project in that language with me, I'd love to hear from you!
